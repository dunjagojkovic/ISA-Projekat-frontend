ABOUT US

It all started with a small office with only three employees, which was founded in 1995. From then until today, for a full 26 years, World Vision Clinic has continuously improved and expanded the range of its services, through cooperation with eminent doctors, constant modernization of medical equipment and investment in the education of its employees. Thanks to that, it is recognized as a regionally relevant medical center. Today, the expert team of World Vision Clinic consists of about 800 doctors, top experts, among whom are some of the biggest names in Serbian medicine.

At six locations, connected by a single call center and an integrated computer network, World Vision Clinic provides reliable and quality medical services - examinations, state-of-the-art diagnostics, surgical treatment, hospital treatment in luxury apartments, dental services, systematic examinations, laboratory services, home visits and transportation of patients from 0 to 24 hours, as well as many other medical services. It has five state-of-the-art operating rooms, the most modern angiosal in Serbia, an intensive care unit, as well as luxury hospital apartments.

In 2016, the first bariatric operation of a child in the region was performed in World Vision Clinic, and for the first time in Serbia, the bladder was removed and the bowel was reconstructed. As a consequence of all these advances, there have been numerous awards and recognitions, among which we emphasize the prestigious European Business Awards for the relationship with the client. Because the main idea of ​​the leader of World Vision Clinic for all 26 years of existence is the PATIENT IN THE CENTER OF ATTENTION. This is confirmed by the fact that more than 500,000 satisfied patients have placed their trust in this institution.

The new hospitals building the future of health infrastructure

In autumn 2020, the Government made a commitment to build 40 hospitals in Serbia by 2030, with a budget of £3.7 billion. In July this year, the Government announced the selection process for a further eight new hospitals. The new sites are intended to transform NHS services, for patients, staff and the wider community.

This quantity of builds taking place over a 10-year period, work must be carried out in a different way than previous new hospitals.

An operation with new robotic technology was successfully performed in Vienna
